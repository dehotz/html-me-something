England

We began our trip flying into London! After sleeping for several hours due to jet lag, we began our journey. Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, Westminster. Also Stonehenge! We also saw plenty of other places but this is just a taste.


Scotland

Easily the most beautiful place I've ever been. We toured the smalled Scotch distillery, Edradour, they have 3 people working there. Followed by driving through the amazing highlands. Oh we also took a dip in Loch Ness, brr it's chilly! No Nessie sighting tho, but we did get to warm up with a bottle of the Edradour. Oh and I broke into a castle! Shhh, don't tell.

Ireland

Second most beautiful place, but wins the prize for the friendliest people around. Even despite our mixing up trains and buses and constantly getting lost. Yes Guiness tastes very different at the brewery. We also had to kiss the blarney stone, despite how gross it must be, I mean the thing gets kissed all day by tourists.

France

Sadly didn't get to spend as much time here as I'd have liked to, only got a few days in Paris. Pro-tip when you know the Eiffel Tower is going to close soon, don't spend 20 minutes taking pictures out in front of it instead of walking up to it. Versailles was amazing and humongous, as was the Louvre. It houses some of the most famous works of art, including of course the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, as well this crazy painting.

Netherlands

Amsterdam, wow, crazy place! A bit surreal for an American to have people just routinely approaching you on the street offering to sell you hard drugs. It's probably a good thing I lost my wallet shortly after arriving here as it certainly kept me out of trouble. Lots of canals, some of them, not pictured, are very interesting. They sure do love bikes, and honest advertising! Hah! Oh, and weed college!


Sweden

Just visited Stockholm here. We stopped into Ice bar, which isn't as cool as the ice hotel but still very neat. Longest day of the year, we were at a boat on a hostel staying up till 3am and playing cards. It's so far north that in the summer the sun never fully goes down just dips and comes back up.

Germany

Getting to visit the Deutches Mueseum of technology and looking at some interesting but super old tech! Speaking of old, I got to stop by the oldest continually operating bierhall in the world, Hofbrahaus. Finished the trip with an incredibly picturesque, at least from the outside, castle Neuschwanstein.
